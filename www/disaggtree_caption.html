Note: Rectangle size represents a place's overall popularity, computed by n Belong + n Don't Belong.

Rectangle color represents a place's inclusiveness, which is a percentage indicating how inclusive a place is:
n Belong / (n Belong + n Don't Belong) * 100.

The color Pink represents the high end of the inclusiveness continuum, and Yellow represents the low end. Yellow text represents inclusiveness > 75% (Greater than 75%).

'Disaggregated' = Nothing was done to combine individual places into larger places (e.g., residence halls into University Housing).

'Less Delimited' = The criterion for inclusion in a treemap was a total click count of at least 20, which is less restrictive than the criterion used in 'More Delimited.'

The criterion for presenting a treemap was at least 4 places represented. Additionally, all treemaps omit clicks on Lawn, Other, and Out of Bounds regions.

Some names of places with lower popularity were abbreviated to fit the text in their respective rectangles:

  • TF = Turf Field
  • DS = Duck Store
  • Barn = Outdoor Program Barn
  • Cas = Cascade
  • FAS = Fine Art Studios
  • MNL = Many Nations Longhouse
  • MS = LERC Military Science
  • MNCH = Museum of Natural and Cultural History
  • Moss = Moss Street Children's Center

Because this overall treemap combines data from multiple academic years, and because both places and methods have changed over time, there is some inaccuracy in representing popularity and inclusiveness.
